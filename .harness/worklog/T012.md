# T012 — WritingCanvas: PKCanvasView wrapper with undo/clear

## Completed

**2026-07-18**

### What I built
- `Sources/Features/Session/Canvas/WritingCanvas.swift`:
  - `WritingCanvasController` (`@MainActor @Observable`): `isDrawingEmpty` (private(set)), a `weak`
    `PKCanvasView` reference, `undo()` (routes through `canvasView.undoManager?.undo()`), `clear()`
    (assigns a fresh `PKDrawing()` and touches nothing else), and `drawingDidChange(isEmpty:)` called
    by the delegate.
  - `WritingCanvas: UIViewRepresentable` — `makeUIView` configures `drawingPolicy = .anyInput`,
    `backgroundColor = .clear` / `isOpaque = false` (so the guide-box layer behind it shows through),
    a single `PKInkingTool(.pen, color: .black, width: Self.penWidth)` (`penWidth = 6`, the sole width
    literal), `accessibilityIdentifier = "writing-canvas"`, and a `UIPencilInteraction` wired to the
    coordinator. No `PKToolPicker` attached anywhere.
  - `Coordinator` conforms to `PKCanvasViewDelegate` (flips `isDrawingEmpty` on
    `canvasViewDrawingDidChange`) and `UIPencilInteractionDelegate` (`pencilInteractionDidTap` →
    `controller.undo()`).
  - `#Preview("Writing Canvas")`: the canvas in a 500×220 frame on `KakitoriTheme.paper` with a thin
    `inkFaint` outline, plus "Undo"/"Clear" buttons (`canvas-undo` / `canvas-clear`) wired to the
    controller.
